Product Features

Why This Series Is Used

Protect Female Threads

Quick clamping reduces repeated screw-in operations and helps protect internal thread surfaces from wear.

Multiple Structural Versions

Button, lever, extended-neck, and pneumatic variants can be reviewed for different port depth and operating-space conditions.

High-Pressure Capability

The series can be evaluated for high-pressure test duties when the exact thread, seal, and fixture conditions are confirmed.

Production-Friendly Sealing

A repeatable push-to-seal workflow helps reduce operator variation and false failures caused by inconsistent threaded hookups.

Operation Logic

Connect by Pressing Instead of Screwing

1. Align with the Female Thread

Align the connector with the internal threaded port and make sure the entrance chamfer and depth are suitable.

2. Press or Actuate

Press the button, handle, or pneumatic actuator so the sealing structure enters and locks to the threaded interface.

3. Test and Release

Run the pressure or leak test, then release the actuator to remove the connector cleanly for the next cycle.

Application Cases

Where G80 Is Usually Evaluated

G80 is a practical route for female threaded ports on housings, valve bodies, manifolds, fixtures, and production test stations.

Valve Body and Manifold Ports

For internal threaded pressure ports on valve bodies, blocks, and machined manifolds.

Production Leak Test Fixtures

For fixtures that need faster and more consistent connection than threaded adapters.

Hydraulic and Pneumatic Parts

For pressure validation on fluid power components with female threaded test points.

Automation Stations

For automated or semi-automated stations where pneumatic actuation and repeatability matter.

For internal threaded ports, the small details matter: thread depth, chamfer, wall thickness, and available access can decide the correct G80 structure.

03

Operation Space
