Sensor Range Choice for Leak Testers

Sensor range should match the real test pressure and required sensitivity. A range that is too wide may make small pressure changes harder to judge; a range that is too narrow may limit future applications.

Resource Overview

Practical Notes for Early Project Review

This resource is adapted from leak testing training material and rewritten for international website use. It is intended as a selection guide, not a final test specification.

Start From Test Pressure

The first question is the normal test pressure. L530-070, L530-035, and L530-010 are standard starting points for 0.7 MPa, 0.35 MPa, and 0.1 MPa sensor ranges.

Balance Range and Sensitivity

Smaller pressure ranges can be useful when the leak limit is tight and the test pressure is low. Higher ranges may be better when the product needs more pressure margin or several similar products share one station.

Confirm With Real Parts

Part volume, material flexibility, stabilization time, and fixture repeatability all affect the final choice. The best range is usually confirmed with sample parts and the target cycle time.

Selection Flow

What to Confirm Before Quoting

These steps help turn a broad leak testing request into a practical model, fixture, and test parameter discussion.

01

Confirm normal test pressure

Tell us the planned test pressure and whether the station needs pressure margin for different parts.

02

Match a standard range

Use L530-070, L530-035, or L530-010 as the first comparison route for differential pressure electronic regulation projects.

03

Review sensitivity and cycle time

Check whether the range can support the required leak limit within a realistic charge, settle, and test time.

04

Validate with fixture and samples

Confirm final judgement using the real sealing method, product volume, and sample parts whenever possible.
