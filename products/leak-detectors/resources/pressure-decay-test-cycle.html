Pressure Decay Test Cycle: Charge, Settle, Test, Exhaust

A pressure decay test is not only the measurement period. Loading, charging, stabilization, test time, and unloading all affect production cycle planning.

Resource Overview

Practical Notes for Early Project Review

This resource is adapted from leak testing training material and rewritten for international website use. It is intended as a selection guide, not a final test specification.

Charge

The part is pressurized to the selected test pressure. Some applications charge slightly above the target to account for stabilization behavior.

Settle

The pressurized part and test volume are allowed to stabilize. Flexible parts, temperature change from compression, and fixture movement can all affect the measured pressure.

Test and Exhaust

During the test period, the instrument measures pressure decay. After judgement, the station exhausts pressure and the operator or automation system unloads the part.
