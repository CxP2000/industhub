Leak Test Fixture Checklist

The fixture is the bridge between the instrument and the real product. A strong tester cannot compensate for unstable sealing, unsupported parts, or unclear test volume.

Resource Overview

Practical Notes for Early Project Review

This resource is adapted from leak testing training material and rewritten for international website use. It is intended as a selection guide, not a final test specification.

Sealing Interface

Confirm whether the part uses a threaded port, tube end, flange, valve seat, connector, or custom sealing surface. The seal must be stable and repeatable before the leak result can be trusted.

Part Support

Flexible parts may expand during pressurization. Heavy, cast, or machined parts may need accurate locating. The fixture should reduce movement and avoid adding unnecessary test volume.

Production Integration

Review loading method, operator access, quick connectors, pass/fail output, data recording, and whether the tester will be bench-mounted or integrated into a station.
