Leak Rate Basics for Production Leak Testing

Leak rate describes how much gas or fluid escapes from, or enters into, a sealed part during a known time period. The right leak limit depends on product function and failure risk.

Resource Overview

Practical Notes for Early Project Review

This resource is adapted from leak testing training material and rewritten for international website use. It is intended as a selection guide, not a final test specification.

What Is a Leak?

A leak is a path that allows contents to escape or outside media to enter. In practice, the question is not whether everything leaks, but what leak level will cause product failure.

Basic Relationship

Leakage can be understood as volume change over time. In pressure decay testing, pressure drop, internal volume, and test time are used together to judge whether the leak is acceptable.

Why Volume and Time Matter

Lower leak rates usually require longer test time. Larger part volume can make small pressure changes harder to detect, so fixture volume and internal test volume should be considered during method selection.
